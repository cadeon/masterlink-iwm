Templates

Description:

Templates are the subsection of the Requirements section which handles the basic policy criteria that are defined in the system. This section houses a hierarchy of component templates which are termed "Object Templates". Within these general requirements for the support of generic objects is defined. For example it may house a hierarchy of mobile equipment, which may be broken down into more specific components as one progresses through the hierarchy structure, ending with very specific types of mobile equipment (i.e. gas powered vans).

The core capability of the Templates structure is its ability to standardize requirements for all resources in the system, and distribute effortlessly that standardization to the desired resources. One important concept to understand about templates is that they are generic. Regardless of how specific the lowest hierarchy level is, it should still remain generic enough so as not to specify a particular resource (i.e. Passenger Car Vs. Fleet Passenger Car # 9). This relationship is considered analogous to that of Nouns and a Proper Nouns in the English Language.

For each classification in the hierarchy one may assign specific requirements such that, for example:

·Specify that all Fueled Equipment require yearly service or every 5000 miles, which ever comes first.  
·Specify that all Fueled Equipment be serviced by a qualified technician.  
·Specify that all Electrical DC Powered Signs be stored indoors.  

These are just examples of the types of requirements, and as evident from the list they may vary in type, frequency, change according to the hierarchy type and level.
